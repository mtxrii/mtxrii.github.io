Hello. My name is Enrique       

While I may be far from an artist, I would still like to think I'm a pretty creative person. My inspiration thrives best, and I find innovation easiest when challenging the conventional ways of doing things. I may not be the best at creating new things, but I really do enjoy reinventing them.

I enjoy coding things. I first got my feet in the water with computer programming back in high school, when I taught myself how to mod video games. Since then, I've used what I learned to make some cool stuff, ace my computer science classes, develop my skills even further, and have fun along the way.

I believe my biggest asset isn't what I know, but what I aspire to know.

In a field as quickly evolving as this one, the ability to constantly pick up and master new skills is a must—which is where my inherent interest in all things software related has come in handy. I not only readily learn anything and everything I need for a project at hand, but I truly enjoy the process of doing so.

I'd like to think a large portion of what makes good software so successful is not only the results it can create, but also the satisfaction and enjoyment of using it. This goes beyond just proper functions and efficient code, instead benefitting from ergonomic UX and elegant interactions.

This is an area I've grown comfortable in, and while my primary expertice may lie in the back end, I do hold my share of experience in proper user engagement, as well as visually appealing design cues.

In many fields, one of the best ways to understand something is by taking it apart and putting it back together. I've found this concept useful when initially confronting a programming task and figuring out a modular, abstract approach. And therefore I've come to grasp a pretty full understanding of object oriented programming, and how it can be applied to basically any representation of data.

And when handling data, anyone can make an inference based off a snapshot of information. But analyzing trends and continuities effectively will show a much bigger picture. In any scenario where analysis of information is needed, I strive to not only create an effective system of gathering the data, but also either present it graphically for human evaluation, or effectively set it up to be evaluated internally.
