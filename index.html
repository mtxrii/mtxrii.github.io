Hello. My name is Enrique Davalos.

I enjoy coding things. I first got my feet in the water with computer programming back in high school, when I taught myself how to mod video games. Since then, I've used what I learned to make some cool stuff, ace my computer science classes, develop my skills even further, and have fun along the way.

I believe my biggest asset isn't what I know, but what I aspire to know.

In a field as quickly evolving as this one, the ability to constantly pick up and master new skills is a must—which is where my inherent interest in all things software related has come in handy. I not only readily learn anything and everything I need for a project at hand, but I truly enjoy the process of doing so.

In many fields, one of the best ways to understand something is by taking it apart and putting it back together. I've found this concept useful when initially confronting a programming task and figuring out a modular, abstract approach. And therefore I've come to grasp a pretty full understanding of object oriented programming, and how it can be applied to basically any representation of data.

And when handling data, anyone can make an inference based off a snapshot of information. But analyzing trends and continuities effectively will show a much bigger picture. In any scenario where analysis of information is needed, I strive to not only create an effective system of gathering the data, but also either present it graphically for human evaluation, or effectively set up to evaluate it internally.
